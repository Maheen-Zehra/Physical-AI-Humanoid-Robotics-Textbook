## Unity in Robotics

Unity is a real-time 3D engine known for high-quality graphics and interaction. In robotics, Unity is used to:
- Create visually realistic environments
- Simulate human-robot interaction
- Generate synthetic training data

---

## Human-Robot Interaction

Unity excels at simulating:
- Human avatars
- Gestures and movement
- Interactive environments

This is valuable for humanoid robots that operate in human-centered spaces.

---

## Visual Fidelity vs Physics

Unity prioritizes realism and interaction, while Gazebo focuses on physics accuracy. Many advanced systems use:
- Gazebo for control and physics
- Unity for visualization and interaction

---

## Use Cases
- Training perception models
- Testing social robotics scenarios
- Demonstrating robot behavior visually
